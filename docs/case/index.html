01 · GTM Reasoning

The math starts at 300 mails per meeting

Generic cold outbound in comparable enterprise cases books one meeting per ~300 mails, about 1% of prospects. Two consequences decide the case:

VOLUME BURNS THE MARKET
  • ~20 meetings/mo needed for €4M → at 300:1 that is ~72,000 mails/yr
  • The whole market holds ~15,000 relevant people (3 personas × 5,000 accounts) → every one of them in a cold sequence every 9 months, forever
  • Contacts in one account share the same "no active project" → meetings scale with accounts in a window, extra mailboxes add mails only
TIMING RAISES YIELD PER MAIL
  • Open buying window (evidenced, this week) removes the biggest failure cause before sending
  • Right person · evidence-grounded relevance · a give with a yes/no ask
  • Stacked working hypothesis: ~8x baseline for the hot tier, ~3x for the scaled tier · measured from month 3

  • Second-order effect: meetings inside a deadline-driven window close above average in a PoC-led motion. Same pipeline euro, more revenue behind it.
02 · The Signal Thesis

Every ECC customer faces a deadline

SAP ends ECC mainstream maintenance in 2027 (2030 extended). Archiving before migration cuts the HANA bill; decommissioning after is mandatory. Detectable, budgeted, deadline-driven: Signal S1.

Tier 1 · buying window open, act within days

05 · Beyond the Cog

The full engine is five layers

The built component is layers 1 to 4 in miniature, for one signal type. The engagement scales it across the taxonomy on the same architecture.

L5 · Feedback

Conversion per signal type → kill or scale. CRO dashboard: pipeline by signal, cost per SQL, SDR time saved.

L4 · Activation

Tier A human-approved 1:1 + LinkedIn · Tier B sequences on warmed domains · Tier C nurture · phone as optional SDR lever.

L3 · Scoring & Routing

Fit × signal → tier, owner, SLA. Both CRMs feed suppression read-only; writes go to HubSpot, already in the stack.

L2 · Signal Detection

Code watchers for German sources (job boards, registers) + Dealfront triggers. Everything normalizes to one signal_event.

L1 · Account Universe

Full TAM (~5k) with fit scores and persisted contacts. Built from ArchivoX's own CRMs first, then Dealfront.

  • Deliberately out of scope: CRM consolidation (flagged to the CRO as its own workstream), custom sequencing, extra UI. The engine is the brain between market and CRM.
  • DACH legal reality shapes the motion: UWG §7 pushes Tier A toward individual, evidence-anchored 1:1 messages at low volume, which is also the motion that converts in enterprise.
